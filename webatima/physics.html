Energy loss

The total energy loss of the projectile is calculated as a sum of electronic and nuclear stopping powers. \begin{equation} \frac{dE}{dx} = \left( \frac{dE}{dx} \right)_n + \left(\frac{dE}{dx} \right)_e \end{equation} $(\frac{dE}{dx})_n$ is the nuclear stopping power and $(\frac{dE}{dx})_e$ is the electronic stopping power

Electronic Stopping Power

\begin{equation} -\frac{dE}{dx} = 4 \pi N_A m_e c^2 r_e^2 \frac{Z_{p}^2}{\beta^2} \frac{Z_{t}}{A_{t}} L \quad [MeV/g/cm^{-2}] \end{equation} $x$ is thickness is in $[g/cm^2]$ units, $r_{e}$ is classical electron radius, $Z_{p}$ is the projectile charge, $A_{p}$ is the mass of the projectile, $Z_{t}$ is the target proton number, $A_{t}$ is the mass number of the target, $\beta$ is the projectile velocity, $m_{e}$ is the electron mass, $N_{A}$ is the Avogadro number

Stopping number L

The stopping number L is calculated according to Lindhard-Sørensen theory as described in [LS].

Nuclear Stopping Power

\begin{equation} S_n(E) = \frac{100 \cdot 8.46210 \cdot Z_{p} Z_{t} A_{p} S_n(\epsilon)}{(A_{p} + A_{t})*(Z_{p}^{0.23} + Z_{t}^{0.23})} \frac{6.022}{A_{t}} \quad [MeV/g/cm^2] \end{equation} \begin{equation} \epsilon = \frac{32.53 A_{t} E}{Z_{p} Z_{t} (A_{p} + A_{t})(Z_{p}^{0.23} + Z_{t}^{0.23})} \end{equation} \begin{equation} S_n(\epsilon) = \frac{ln(1+1.1383 \epsilon)}{2(\epsilon + 0.01321\epsilon^{0.21226} + 0.19593\epsilon^{0.5})} \quad \epsilon \leq 30 \end{equation} \begin{equation} S_n(\epsilon) = \frac{ln(\epsilon)}{2\epsilon} \end{equation}

Effective Charge

In the calculation of electronics stopping power the effective charge of the projectile is used (if not specified otherwise) instead of proton number of projectile. The following formulas are implemented:

  • Pierce-Blann: $Z_{eff} = Z_{p} \left[ 1-exp(-\frac{0.95 \beta}{\alpha Z_{p}^{2/3}}) \right]$
  • GLOBAL: mean charge from GLOBAL code [global]
  • Winger: mean charge from [Winger]
  • Atima v1.4: combination of above, see [atima]

Range

The continuous slowing down approximation is used to calculate range of the projectile from the calculated total stopping power: \begin{equation} R(E) = \int \frac{dE}{dE/dx} \end{equation}

Stopping Power in Compounds

The Bragg rule is used to calculate the stopping power for compounds: \begin{equation} \frac{dE}{dx} =\sum w_i \frac{dE}{dx} i \end{equation} $w_i$ weight fraction of the element, $dE/dx i$ is total energy loss of the element in $MeV/g/cm^2$
